BUREAU OF FIRE PROTECTION - STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURES (FIRE RESPONSE)

1. INITIAL CONTACT AND INFORMATION GATHERING
Upon receipt of a fire call, the dispatcher must obtain:
- Exact location of the fire (Street, Barangay, Landmark).
- Nature of the fire (Residential, Commercial, Industrial, Grass fire).
- Presence of trapped persons or injuries.
- Callback number of the informant.

2. PRE-ARRIVAL INSTRUCTIONS FOR CALLERS
- Evacuation: Instruct the caller to evacuate immediately if they are in danger. Do not use elevators.
- Containment: If safe, close doors and windows to slow the spread of fire.
- Utilities: Instruct the caller to shut off electricity and gas if possible before leaving.
- Safe Meeting Point: Advise callers to meet at a designated safe area away from the structure.
- Do Not Re-enter: Strictly prohibit callers from re-entering a burning structure for any reason.

3. DISPATCH PROTOCOLS
- Alert the nearest fire station immediately.
- Dispatch at least two (2) fire engines for structural fires.
- Notify EMS/Ambulance if injuries are reported.
