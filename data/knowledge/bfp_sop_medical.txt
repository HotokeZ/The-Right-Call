BUREAU OF FIRE PROTECTION - STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURES (EMERGENCY MEDICAL SERVICES)

1. FIRST AID PROCEDURES
- Airway: Ensure the patient's airway is clear.
- Breathing: Check for signs of breathing and pulse.
- Bleeding: If there is severe bleeding, instruct the caller to apply direct pressure to the wound with a clean cloth.
- Burn Treatment: For minor burns, cool with running water for 10-20 minutes. Do not apply ice, butter, or ointments.
- Shock: Keep the patient warm and lying down. Elevate legs slightly if no head or neck injury is suspected.

2. CPR GUIDELINES
- If the patient is not breathing and has no pulse, instruct the caller to perform Hands-Only CPR.
- Rate: 100-120 compressions per minute.
- Depth: At least 2 inches.

3. PATIENT SAFETY
- Do not move the patient unless there is an immediate threat to life (e.g., fire, explosion).
- Keep the patient calm until the ambulance arrives.
